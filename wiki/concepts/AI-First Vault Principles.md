# AI-First Vault Principles (Korg Edition)

## The 7 Rules

1. **Self-contained context**  
   Every note explains itself.

2. **`## For future Grok` preamble**  
   2–4 sentence summary immediately after frontmatter so Grok can decide relevance fast.

3. **Rich, consistent frontmatter**  
   `date`, `type`, `tags`, `ai-first: true`, `status`, plus type-specific fields.

4. **Recency markers per claim**  
   External facts get `(as of YYYY-MM, source)` inline.

5. **Sources preserved verbatim**  
   URLs and key quotes are never paraphrased.

6. **Mandatory [[wikilinks]]**  
   Every referenced entity, project, concept, or decision gets linked.

7. **Never write in isolation (Propagation)**  
   Every meaningful write also touches the relevant project note, today's daily note, and `log.md`.

## Why This Matters for Grok Work

- Long agent sessions (implement-review loops, multi-wave orchestrators, skill development) produce dozens of insights that normally get lost.
- These rules + good skills (`korg-challenge`, `korg-reconcile`, `korg-synthesize`) turn the vault into a true compounding asset instead of a growing pile of notes.

## Relationship to obsidian-second-brain

We are heavily inspired by the system but adapting it to:
- Native Grok strengths (subagents, direct tool use)
- Our existing `cli-anything-obsidian` foundation
- A slightly more human-readable tone while keeping the agent advantages
