# Product Overview

**Korg / Yvaeh Mode** is a cutting‑edge, adversarial multi‑agent orchestration framework that transforms a single CLI prompt into a self‑compounding swarm of intelligent agents. Designed for developers, researchers, and enterprises seeking autonomous, scalable reasoning, Korg delivers:

- **Multi‑persona swarm** – Four distinct agents (Captain, Harper, Benjamin, Lucas) collaborate and compete to explore solution spaces.
- **Live contract negotiation** – Dynamic BERT‑based similarity scoring refines proposals in real time.
- **Fault‑tolerant execution** – Persistent blackboard and `.ktrans` transaction logs guarantee crash recovery and state continuity.
- **Automatic knowledge enrichment** – Post‑run `reconcile` and `synthesize` steps automatically generate new documentation and backlinks, making the system smarter with every use.
- **Premium UI** – A glass‑morphism Ratatui dashboard with gauges, sparklines, and interactive modals provides an immersive command‑line experience.

### Market Positioning
Korg bridges the gap between **large‑language‑model‑driven brainstorming** and **production‑grade tooling**. It enables teams to:
- Rapidly prototype complex workflows without manual orchestration.
- Capture and retain institutional knowledge automatically.
- Scale reasoning effort from a single developer to a 16‑agent swarm on demand.

### Value Proposition
| Stakeholder | Pain Point | Korg Solution |
|-------------|------------|---------------|
| **Developers** | Repetitive boilerplate for orchestrating multi‑step AI pipelines. | One‑liner `korg "<prompt>"` launches the full lifecycle. |
| **Researchers** | Difficulty reproducing and extending AI experiments. | Persistent blackboard + `.ktrans` logs provide full reproducibility. |
| **Product Teams** | Knowledge decay as projects evolve. | Automatic `synthesize` enriches the knowledge vault after each run. |
